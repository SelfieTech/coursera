About Audi-A3

The Audi A3 is one of the most affordable Audis is available in Indian market. The A3 boasts of stylish exteriors accentuated by sleek bill, shapely headlamps, and rising shoulder line. The quality of the interiors is plush and the car offers sufficient space for five occupants. Under the Hood, Audi has offered two engine options - a 145PS 2.0-liter diesel mated to 6-speed automatic transmission and 150PS 1.4-liter petrol engine mated to 7-speed automatic gearbox. The ride and handling combination of Audi A3 is one among the key highlights of this executive sedan while on safety front, you do get dual airbags and rear parking sensors as a part of the standard package. In the Indian market, the Audi A3 competes against the likes of Mercedes Benz A-class and CLA-class and BMW 3-series among others.



Audi-A3 OverView

Audi India has slashed the prices of its A3 model range by around Rs 5 lakh. This comes as a result of Audi celebrating 5 years of A3 model in India. All four variants of the A3 are now cheaper by around Rs 3.58 lakh to Rs 4.95 lakh. The 2.0-litre TDI diesel engine produces 143PS/320Nm mated to a 6-speed automatic gearbox while the 1.4-litre TFSI petrol engine makes 150PS and 250Nm of torque paired with a 7-speed automatic gearbox. The A3 in India comes loaded with various features like panoramic sunroof, wireless charging, 2 zone climate control air conditioner and more. The Audi A3 in India faces competition in the form of Mercedes Benz CLA in India. The Audi A3 in India is priced between Rs 28.99 lakh to Rs 31.99 lakh (ex-showroom, Mumbai).



Personal OverView

ADVANTAGES

  • Premium interior with great fit and finish quality.
  • Long list of features – LED headlights and tail lights, dynamic turn indicators, Audi Multi-Media Interface, panoramic sunroof etc.
  • Both engines are fuel-efficient and offer adequate performance.
  • Classy, understated design and rock solid build quality.

DIS-ADVANTAGES

  • Cabin space, while better than its rival, the Mercedes CLA’s, is quite limited for the price.
  • No paddle-shifters offered.
  • Dummy buttons on centre console and passenger doors look ungainly.
