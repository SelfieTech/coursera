About BMW X7

BMW India has launched the top-spec X7 M50d at Rs 1.63 crore. It’s Rs 56,000 more expensive than the xDrive40i M Sport, the variant below it. Considering it’s an M variant, the X7 M50d naturally gets plenty of M badging, sportier bumpers, large 22-inch alloy wheels, and an updated steering wheel. The M50d gets a beast of an engine in the form of a 3.0-litre quad-turbocharged diesel mill, which puts out 400PS and 760Nm, giving it the ability to achieve 0-100 kmph in just 5.4 seconds. An 8-speed automatic transmission drives all four wheels. Features include a 12.3-inch touchscreen infotainment system with Apple CarPlay and gesture controls, a three-part panoramic sunroof, ambient lighting, laser LED headlights, soft-close doors, reclining seats for all three rows, a 5-zone climate control, and a Bowers and Wilkins sound system. The BMW X7 M50d locks horns with the Porsche Cayenne, Audi Q8, Range Rover Sport, and the upcoming Mercedes-Benz GLS.
